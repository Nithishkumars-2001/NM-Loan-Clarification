What exactly is a home loan?

Quick take: Home loans offer the lowest interest rates among all loan types because they are backed by a physical, high-value asset - the house itself.

A home loan is a secured loan given by a bank or housing finance company to help you purchase, construct, or renovate a residential property. "Secured" means the property itself becomes the collateral - the lender holds a legal claim (a mortgage) on it until you repay the entire loan, principal and interest.

Because the loan is secured, home loans usually carry lower interest rates than personal loans and offer far longer repayment tenures, often stretching from 10 to 30 years. Lenders are willing to offer these easier terms because their risk is covered: if you stop repaying, they can legally recover their money by selling the mortgaged property through a foreclosure process.

Most banks finance up to 75-90% of the property's value, depending on the loan amount slab. The remaining portion, known as the down payment or margin money, has to come from your own savings. This ratio - loan amount divided by property value - is called the Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio, and it's one of the first numbers you should understand before house-hunting.

Interest on home loans can be fixed, floating, or a hybrid of both. A fixed rate stays the same for a set period regardless of what happens in the broader economy, giving you predictable EMIs. A floating rate moves up or down with the lender's benchmark rate (commonly linked to the repo rate set by the central bank), which means your EMI - or your tenure - can change over time. Most borrowers today choose floating rates because they tend to average out cheaper over a long tenure, but they come with the discomfort of uncertainty.

Who is eligible for a home loan?

Criteria Typical Requirement
Age 21-65 years at loan maturity (varies by lender)
Employment Salaried with 2+ years experience, or self-employed with stable ITR history
Minimum income Usually ₹25,000/month for salaried applicants in metro cities
Credit score 750+ preferred for the best interest rate; 650+ often still qualifies
Existing debt Lenders check your Debt-to-Income ratio, ideally under 40-50%
Property type Must be legally clear title, approved by local municipal authority

How the home loan process actually works

  1. Check your eligibility and get a rough sanction estimate using a lender's online calculator before you finalise a property.
  2. Shortlist the property and get a sale agreement or builder-buyer agreement in place.
  3. Submit your loan application along with income proof, identity proof, and property documents.
  4. The lender conducts a technical valuation (to confirm the property's market worth) and a legal check (to confirm clear title, no disputes, and proper approvals).
  5. Based on your income and the valuation, the lender issues a sanction letter specifying the approved amount, rate, and tenure.
  6. On disbursement day, the loan amount is released - either fully (for a ready property) or in stages (for an under-construction property, tied to construction milestones).
  7. EMIs begin the month after disbursement, and the property remains mortgaged to the lender until the full loan, including interest, is repaid.

Documents you'll typically need

  • PAN card and Aadhaar card
  • Last 3 months' salary slips
  • Form 16 or income tax returns (2-3 years)
  • Bank statements (6 months)
  • Property sale agreement
  • Title deed and encumbrance certificate
  • Approved building plan
  • Passport-size photographs
Watch out: Always ask for the Annual Percentage Rate (APR), not just the headline interest rate - processing fees, legal charges, and insurance add-ons can quietly push your real borrowing cost up by 0.5-1.5%.
