How does a gold loan work?

Quick take: Gold loans are one of the few loan types where your own credit score often plays little to no role in approval - the collateral does the talking.

A gold loan is a secured loan where you pledge your gold jewellery or coins as collateral in exchange for funds. The lender - typically a bank or a specialised gold loan NBFC (Non-Banking Financial Company) - keeps your gold in secure custody for the duration of the loan and returns it to you once you repay in full.

The amount you can borrow depends on the Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio set by regulation, which caps how much of the gold's assessed market value can be lent out - commonly up to 75% of the value for most standard gold loans. The lender determines the value based on the purity (karat) and weight of the gold, using standardised valuation methods, not sentimental or antique value of any jewellery design.

Gold loans are known for their speed because there's minimal paperwork - no income proof, no credit score check in many cases, no property valuation. The gold itself is the security, so approval and disbursement can happen within a few hours of your gold being appraised, making it a popular option for urgent, short-term cash needs.

Repayment structures are flexible compared to other loans. Common options include regular EMIs (principal plus interest), paying only interest periodically with the principal due at the end (bullet repayment), or even paying the entire interest and principal together at maturity. This flexibility, combined with speed, is what makes gold loans distinct - but it also means interest can compound if you're not careful with a bullet repayment structure.

How a gold loan is disbursed

  1. Carry your gold jewellery or coins along with KYC documents to the lender's branch.
  2. The lender's certified appraiser evaluates the purity and weight of the gold in your presence.
  3. Based on the current market rate and the applicable LTV cap, the lender calculates the maximum loan amount.
  4. You choose a repayment structure - regular EMI, interest-only with bullet principal, or full bullet repayment.
  5. Once you accept the terms, the gold is sealed, recorded, and securely stored, and funds are disbursed - often on the same day.
  6. On full repayment, the gold is returned to you after verification; if you default, the lender can initiate the auction process after due notice.

Documents typically required

  • PAN card
  • Aadhaar card or other government ID
  • Address proof
  • Passport-size photograph
  • Gold ownership is generally assumed, not separately certified
Watch out: If you default and the lender auctions your gold, any amount recovered above your outstanding dues is legally supposed to be returned to you - but auction prices can be lower than market value, so defaulting often means a real financial loss beyond just losing the jewellery.
